LESLY MELENDEZ
I created a series of publications under a brand called Melon Magazine that speak upon my experiences of what it means to be Hispanic from my personal perspective through bookmaking, storytelling, and multimedia experimentation. This series is meant to share my story and perspective on what it's like to be a Salvadorian woman that has never really felt connected to my own culture. Through these books you will follow along my journey on how I found my way back to myself and the wonderful people that have guided me along the way. We will laugh, we will cry, and at times we will cringe, but it's all love over here!
MAC WOOLLEY
For Ever For Real is a movie I have been making over the past few months. It is about the desire for scientific everlasting life on earth. Eternal life has traditionally been sought after through religious promises of the afterlife, but now we can use science to achieve that goal. The movie uses this as the backdrop for more specific performances of health obsession.
MADISON TRAN
Viet Ingredients is a two part project consisting of a publicly accessible, web based archive of Vietnamese ingredient packaging (vietingredients.com) and Nước Chấm, a scrapbook style publication that compiles my own written memories and familial anecdotes pertinent to my identity as an American person of Vietnamese descent.
ARIADNA PEREZ
Market Marvel is a brand that produces products that parody grocery store items, based on my experience of working in a grocery store. To relieve myself from boredom and stress, I've become fixated on elements within where I worked: food packaging, fresh produce and meat, and pre-made food. I've translated this admiration into a line of products that reference these aspects. This line includes sandwich notebooks, fish patches, produce stickers, and food package shirts. Through this work, I share that admiration with my audience and allow them to take a piece of that experience with them.
VY DOAN
Viva Ca Phe celebrates Vietnamese coffee culture through artistically designed packaging and an informative booklet that guides enthusiasts on creating authentic Vietnamese brews. This project highlights unique aspects of Vietnamese coffee, such as robusta beans and the traditional drip brew, and integrates them with elements of traditional Vietnamese art. In addition, I have designed a distinct Vietnamese coffee brand to honor and share Vietnam's rich coffee heritage with a global audience, making it accessible through educational insights and captivating designs. Viva Ca Phe seeks to bridge cultures, inviting the world to explore the depth and tradition of Vietnamese coffee.
